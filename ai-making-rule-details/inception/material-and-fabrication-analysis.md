# Material And Fabrication Analysis

Purpose: identify how material behavior and fabrication method control the design.

## Analyze

- Material type
- Actual thickness
- Thickness variation
- Grain/flute/layer direction
- Rigidity and flexibility
- Minimum cut radius
- Kerf
- Tool limits
- Adhesive or fastener strategy
- Heat, burn, delamination, cracking, warping, shrinkage, or support needs
- Surface finish
- Repeated-use durability

## Common Guidance

- Paper/card/hardboard: prefer broad glue surfaces, large tabs, layering, simple curves, and beginner templates.
- Acrylic: account for brittleness, laser kerf, sharp edges, and stress cracks.
- Plywood: account for grain, burn, kerf, and splintering.
- 3D print: account for layer direction, overhang, tolerances, support, and print orientation.
- Sheet metal: account for bend radius, bend order, burrs, and fastener access.

Create `making-docs/inception/materials/material-fabrication-analysis.md`.
